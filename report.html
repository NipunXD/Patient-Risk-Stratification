The Challenge: Unlocking Clinical Insights

The High Cost of Readmissions

Unplanned hospital readmissions are a major burden on the healthcare system, leading to billions in annual costs and indicating potential gaps in patient care. Identifying "high-risk" patients before discharge is a critical strategy for intervention.

Core Model Performance

Qualitative Test Accuracy

The model achieved **9/10 (90%) accuracy** on our qualitative test set, correctly identifying both high and low-risk patients from complex notes.

Test Note Risk Score Analysis

This chart shows the model's predicted risk score for each of the 10 test notes. The color indicates the model's final verdict (High or Low Risk). Note 7 was the only incorrect prediction.

Key Insight: A "Blind Spot" for Social Factors

Our single failure (Note 7) was the most important finding. It revealed a critical limitation: the model is an expert **clinical analyst** but is "blind" to **Social Determinants of Health (SDoH)**.

Future Work & Conclusion

Proposed Future Work: Hybrid Model

To address the "SDoH blind spot," the next step is to create a hybrid model. This model would combine the rich vector from ClinicalBERT with structured EMR data (like `lives_alone: true`) for a complete, holistic patient risk profile.

Conclusion

This project was a complete success. We built a fully functional, end-to-end AI application that solves a real-world medical problem. We successfully:

  • Identified and implemented a state-of-the-art NLP model (ClinicalBERT).
  • Wrapped this "engine" in a robust, scalable Flask API.
  • Designed and built a modern and professional Next.js frontend that is both visually clear and highly usable.
  • Rigorously evaluated our model and identified its precise limitations, charting a clear path for future improvements.

This project demonstrates the power of combining deep learning with thoughtful system design to create tools that can genuinely augment clinical decision-making.
